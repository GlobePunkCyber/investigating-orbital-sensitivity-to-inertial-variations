Title:
Position Error Accumulation Analysis

Objective:
Evaluate how small artificial changes in the inertia parameter affect satellite position over time.

Parameters:
Lower Inertia = 0.999
Normal Inertia = 1.000
Higher Inertia = 1.001
Duration = 30 Days

Observation:
Position error increased steadily and reached approximately 13,500 km near Day 16 before decreasing.

Interpretation:
Small changes in the inertia parameter produced relatively small orbital radius differences but significant positional divergence due to phase drift along the orbit.
