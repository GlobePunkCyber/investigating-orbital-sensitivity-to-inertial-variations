Title:
Long-Term Sensitivity Analysis

Objective:
Evaluate how orbital position error changes over longer simulation durations.

Parameters:
Lower Inertia = 0.999
Normal Inertia = 1.000
Higher Inertia = 1.001

Simulation Durations:
30 Days
90 Days
180 Days
365 Days

Results:
Maximum position error remained approximately 13,500 km.

Final position error increased from approximately 2,800 km at 30 days to approximately 12,500 km at 365 days.

Observation:
Small artificial changes in the inertia parameter produced large positional divergence over time. The error growth appears to approach a limit rather than increasing indefinitely.
