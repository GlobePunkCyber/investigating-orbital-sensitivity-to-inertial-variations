Experiment 006 – Real GPS Orbit Geometry Analysis

Objective

Evaluate the orbital characteristics of real GPS satellites and establish a real-world reference dataset for future orbital sensitivity studies.

Method

Historical orbital data was obtained from CelesTrak for three operational GPS satellites:

* GPS BIIRM-6 (NORAD 32711)
* GPS BIIF-7 (NORAD 40105)
* GPS BIIF-10 (NORAD 40730)

The following orbital parameters were analyzed:

* Eccentricity
* Semi-Major Axis (SMA)
* Long-term orbital stability

Results

The satellites exhibited distinct orbital geometries:

* GPS BIIF-7 maintained the lowest average eccentricity (~0.003).
* GPS BIIF-10 maintained a moderate average eccentricity (~0.010).
* GPS BIIRM-6 maintained the highest average eccentricity (~0.020).

Despite these differences, all satellites maintained highly stable semi-major axes near 20,180 km over the analysis period.

Conclusion

Operational GPS satellites occupy different but stable orbital geometries. The observed eccentricity differences provide a useful real-world basis for future orbital sensitivity experiments. Results demonstrate that GPS constellation members can maintain long-term orbital stability while operating with varying orbital shapes.
